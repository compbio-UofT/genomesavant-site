Analytics

The final subsection of the "Variants" subsection, "Analytics", is bundled with two Apps for further analyses of the variant data: Enrichment, and Family Matters.

Finding Enrichment

The enrichment analysis App counts the number of variants with a particular property, similar to the charting tool covered in the previous tutorial. However, the properties supported by the Enrichment app are loaded from external databases and take longer to compute. Variants may be aggregated based on a Region List, Family, Cohort, or Ontology. To demonstrate, consider an analysis based on the gene ontology (GO), performed by choosing "Ontology" from the "Aggregate varaints by" drop-down near the top left of the Enrichment App pane, and then choosing "GO - Gene Ontology" from the Ontology drop-down (). This presents a table of expandable rows that functions similarily to that of the Family and Cohort tools found in the Spreadsheet section. Each row can be expanded () or collapsed () to navigate the GO Hierarchy, and the total number of variants that occur within genes possessing the corresponding annotation in each row is given in the "Variant Count" column.

Portion of a GO enrichment analysis showing, for each row, the number of variants that lie within genes that have the corresponding annotation. For example, here 215 variants are associated with neuron cell death.

In addition to the GO ontology, MedSavant includes support for the Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man (OMIM) and Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO) databases of gene-disease associations.
